Hey, I'm Igor. I build AI products for e-commerce at [Sirv](https://sirv.com).

I created [Sirv AI Studio](/projects/sirv-studio/) and built it end to end — merchants scan their Shopify catalog, fix product content in AI batches, route supplier uploads through review, and publish safely with versioning and rollback. React 19, TanStack Start, PostgreSQL, Inngest, Stripe, fal.ai — the whole thing, from schema to billing.

I've always been building things — the [projects page](/projects/) goes back years. Marketing was just one of the skills I picked up along the way, and it turned out to be a useful one: a decade of SEO and growth for an image platform is a very good lens on what merchants actually struggle with. What changed recently isn't that I build — it's how much one person can build. I wrote about that in [Two theories of a programmer](/posts/two-theories-of-a-programmer/): the old theory says a programmer types the code they thought of; the new one says a programmer exercises editorial judgment over a fleet that types. I practice the second theory daily — Claude Fable as auditor and reviewer, sandboxed executors doing the typing, executable review gates in between. Some of that workflow is [open source](https://github.com/IgorVaryvoda/improve-codex).

On this site I write about fleet coding, e-commerce imagery, and SEO — plus the occasional [re-audit of my own old advice](/posts/image-seo-2026/). On the side I build [tools that solve my own problems](/projects/): a travel destination roulette, an AI finance tracker, spaced repetition for Balkan languages.

I'm Ukrainian. Russia's war against my country is still going, and help is still needed — [here's how](https://www.uahelp.me).
